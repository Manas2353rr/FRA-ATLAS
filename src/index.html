About Forest Rights Act

Understanding the Forest Rights Act, 2006 and its implementation

What is the Forest Rights Act?

The Scheduled Tribes and Other Traditional Forest Dwellers (Recognition of Forest Rights) Act, 2006, commonly known as the Forest Rights Act (FRA), is a key piece of legislation in India that recognizes the rights of forest-dwelling communities over land and forest resources.

Key Provisions

  • Individual Forest Rights (IFR): Rights to cultivate and occupy forest land (up to 4 hectares) that the claimant has been cultivating prior to December 13, 2005.
  • Community Rights (CR): Rights to access, use, and collect minor forest produce, grazing areas, water bodies, and traditional knowledge.
  • Community Forest Resource Rights (CFR): Rights to protect, regenerate, conserve, and manage community forest resources within traditional boundaries.

Implementation Challenges

Despite its transformative potential, FRA implementation faces challenges including scattered non-digitized records, lack of centralized monitoring, and limited integration with development schemes.
